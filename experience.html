My Previous Work and Educational Experiences

Segway Robotics (May-July 2017)

Beijing, China

Segway Robotics is the Advanced Personal Robotics (APR) division of Segway. The APR Division in Beijing consists of over 70 full-time engineers performing research and development of Loomo, the world's next innovative personal robot assistant.

I had the honour of working with the Electrical Engineering team, with whom I learned about the entire PCB development cycle, from drawing initial schematics using the Cadence OrCAD tool to developing the PCB layout to the mounting of components by hand. I also worked extensively with programming the microcontroller used by Loomo, the STM32F10x family of MCUs, from generating source code with the STMCubeMX software to download, while using the Keil Microvision IDE.

1B Study Term, University of Waterloo (January-April 2017)

In my second term of study, I took courses in calculus (multivariate and approximations), physics (electromagnetism), digital circuits and logic, engineering design for embedded systems, and discrete math. In labs, I worked with programming Intel MAX10 FPGAs with VHDL using Altera Quartus. I also worked with Android Studio and Java to create my own application for Android phones.

1A Study Term, University of Waterloo (September-December 2016)

In my first term of study, I took foundational courses in calculus, physics (kinematics and dynamics), linear circuits, introductory programming, and chemistry. In labs and projects, I worked with C++ and gained a basic understanding of code structure, debugging, and syntax.

Coming up: 2A Study Term, University of Waterloo (September-December 2017)

In my upcoming third term of study, I will take six courses in microelectric circuits, data structures and algorithms, digital computers, engineering law and ethics, linear algebra, and advanced calculus. In labs and projects, I will work with Assembly code and C++, as well as MATLAB and PSpice.
