My Story

When I was in kindergarten, my kindergarten teacher asked me, "Andrew, what do you want to be when you grow up?". I replied to her, "I want to be the Prime Minister". She gave me a chuckle, patted my head, and said, "Good for you, I'll vote for you."

In senior year of high school, my career advisor asked me, "Andrew, what do you want to be when you graduate?". I replied to him, "I want to be the Prime Minister".

He gave me a chuckle and said, "Why don't you choose something more realistic? How about Software Engineering?".

I was born and raised in Vancouver, Canada where I attended elementary and secondary school. Ever since I was a little child, I was interested in news and current events. As I entered high school, I became more politically involved, volunteering with my Member of Parliament and his political party as well as attending several youth political forums.

Thus, it made sense that I decided to go to McGill University and study in their Bachelor of Arts and Science program for a BASc - a Bachelor's of Arts and Science - degree. I had applied to, and got accepted to the program in McGill. I paid my tuition deposit, began looking for residence in Montreal, and even took AP French in high school.

Then three days before the deadline, I switched to Waterloo Computer Engineering, a back-up choice since applications to Ontario schools had to be made in batches of three.

The two things I told myself that I'd have nothing to do with in university were computers (I hate technology) and engineering (other people can solve these problems for me). So it makes sense now that I'm in Waterloo Computer Engineering.
